Executive Storyline · SCQR

Aptive is choosing to become a different company. The culture has to make the leap with it.

Situation

Where things stand

A half-billion-dollar business built on direct-to-home grit. Operations is the beating heart, and the people care.

Complication

What changed

The old goal of selling and cashing out is retired. A PE transition, a wealth event, and talent loss now pull at grit and identity, while silos keep the company from acting as one.

Question

What we must answer

What gives Aptive one ambition strong enough to break silos, hold talent, answer doubters, and reach the field, not just the home office?

Resolution

Our answer

Build Aptive's Statement of Ambition and Success Factors, then operationalize both into one unifying North Star.

Recommendation · Bottom Line First

Build two products that give Aptive a North Star: a Statement of Ambition and Aptive's Success Factors.

The recommendation

Create the Statement of Ambition and Aptive's Success Factors, the two deliverables that give the company one unifying North Star. The Statement names Aptive's aspiration. The Success Factors name the behaviors that bring the ambition to life.

Reason 01

Leaders need a teachable point-of-view

A great coach knows what good looks like and has the language to teach how to get there. Aptive's leaders do not yet share that language.

Reason 02

Ambition is aspired to, not just observed

A Statement of Ambition names what Aptive reaches for. Values that only describe what already exists fail to invite change. Aspiration sets the bar higher.

Reason 03

The pen is mightier than the sword

A written standard gives people something to rally around, and gives leaders the behavioral framing to hold each other accountable.

Decision

Approve the work to build Aptive's Statement of Ambition and Success Factors, then choose how far to carry it into the field. The three engagement tiers follow.

Our Point of View

This is a unifying behavioral contract, not an internal PR campaign.

Governing conviction

The work earns its power as a behavioral contract the organization helps write, connects to behavior, and lives out in the operation.

Conviction A

Involvement is the first step to buy-in

  • Leaders own what they help build
  • Interviews surface real language, not borrowed slogans
  • Co-creation earns the right to hold people to it
Conviction B

The language must connect to behaviors

  • Ambition without behavior is just a poster
  • Success Factors turn ideals into what people do
  • Behavior is what leaders can coach and correct
Conviction C

Success lives in activation and integration

  • The work lands only when it enters daily operations
  • The field cascade is where risk and payoff sit
  • Integration into the operation is what makes it stick
